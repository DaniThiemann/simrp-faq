SIMRP Plan — Employee FAQ

Quick answers to common questions about the SIMRP employee benefits program.

General

What is the SIMRP Plan?
+

The SIMRP Plan is a supplemental benefits program designed to reduce medical costs and add valuable benefits for employees and their families.

Does this affect my paycheck or take-home pay?
+

No. Oftentimes, the employee’s net take-home pay is slightly increased as a result of implementing our plan due to leftover tax savings.

Was this approved by my employer?
+

Yes. Your employer worked diligently to bring this plan to their employees to help reduce medical costs and add valuable benefits for you and your family.

Am I required to participate?
+

Yes. You are required to participate for one year. After your first year, you can opt out if you are not satisfied with the benefits.

Coverage & Benefits

Does this replace my health insurance?
+

No. This is not a replacement for your health insurance. These benefits stack on top of and complement anything you already have in place. If an employee does not have insurance, this can act as a stand-alone.

What’s included in the plan?
+
  • Virtual Urgent Care – Unlimited
  • Virtual Primary Care – 12 visits per enrolled household member per year
  • Prescription Coverage – Most common prescriptions at no cost (1,000+ covered)
  • Mental Health Counseling – 12 visits per enrolled household member per year
  • Additional wellness resources through the benefits platform
What kind of prescriptions are included?
+

Most common prescriptions (1,000+) are covered at no cost. Specialty or brand-only drugs may vary based on availability.

Potential benefits for employees enrolled
+

Subject to sufficient tax savings, employees enrolled may receive:

  • MEC plan offering 3 in-person primary care visits per year*
  • Hospital bill eraser*
  • Group whole life insurance policy*

*Applicable if enough tax savings are realized. In some cases, the whole life policy may fully subsidize the enrolled employee.

Will I get an insurance card?
+

No insurance card is needed. You’ll access your benefits through the online platform and/or by phone.

Do I have to use certain doctors or providers?
+

No. Virtual services are available nationwide and not limited to a specific network.

Can I use these benefits if I move to another state?
+

Yes. These benefits are available nationwide.

Can I use these benefits while traveling?
+

Yes. Virtual visits and prescription coverage are available anywhere in the U.S., even if you’re away from your home state.

Do the benefits expire at the end of the year?
+

No. These benefits continue as long as you remain enrolled and your employer keeps the program in place.

Dependents & Eligibility

How many family members can I add?
+

You can add up to 6 dependents, including your spouse.

Can I change my dependents during the year?
+

Yes. You can update dependents during open enrollment or after a qualifying life event (marriage, birth, adoption, etc.).

Why do only some employees receive a life insurance policy?
+

The life insurance benefit is determined by available tax allotment within the program. Because every employee’s situation is different, not everyone will have enough allotment to include life insurance in their coverage package. Those who qualify will be notified directly with their policy information.

Access & Usage

Do I need to fill out any forms to get started?
+

No complicated paperwork is required. You will be auto-enrolled in our program.

Do I need internet access to use the benefits?
+

For virtual services, yes — either internet or a phone connection is needed. For in-person MEC plan visits, internet access isn’t required.

Legal, Privacy & Accounts

Does this affect my eligibility for HSA or FSA accounts?
+

No. This plan works alongside HSAs and FSAs and doesn’t interfere with your contributions or usage. If you have an HSA account, we will ensure you are enrolled properly to avoid disrupting anything.

Will my personal health information be shared with my employer?
+

No. Your medical details are confidential and protected under HIPAA. Your employer sees general participation data, not private health records.

What happens if I leave the company?
+

Your coverage under this plan will end when your employment ends, unless your new employer offers a similar program.

Can I continue my life insurance policy if I leave the company?
+

Yes, you can opt into paying out-of-pocket to continue the policy. Most employees choose to continue due to the cash accrual acting as a savings account.
